Background Information: I am Savina Chan, a sophomore at New York University, College of Arts and Science, majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Business Studies. I was raised in Medford, NY to immigrant parents of Chinese descent. As a first-generation student, I was fortunate enough to receive a full ride scholarship (Dean’s Scholarship) and be accepted into the Presidential Honors Scholars Program (top ten percent of the first-year class) at my dream college. I am happy because I have achieved the main purpose of my family's immigration, which was for me to get an education. This accomplishment brings me back to my childhood and memories of working at my family's Chinese restaurant. My parents worked tirelessly and inspired me to do the same, so I began working there every week to earn money for my first phone. Now that I am in college, I still help out at the restaurant occasionally. This experience has given me a deep sense of sonder, which was the basis of my personal essay for college admissions. It is the realization that everyone has their own unique story and experiences. This understanding drives my curiosity and desire to learn from others by asking about their lives and hearing their stories.


Aspirations: While I initially wanted to become a physician assistant, my desire to become a software engineer resurfaced, and I decided to switch to computer science for my second year of college. Although I am a year behind in my CS courses, I have been working hard to teach myself and gain a competitive edge. Technology has always interested me, and I often serve as IT support for my less tech-savvy family and friends. My passion for CS started when I was 10 years old and became engrossed in a game called “MapleStory.” It provoked me into creating my own private server and website by using online guides and a C++ template. To this day, computer science is the door that opens the exploration of both my interests: solving problems and assembling innovative experiences. By means of code, I can develop ideas that unravel user-specific problems, design possible solutions, and then execute them. Ultimately, this end-to-end experience satisfies me because I get to not only imagine an experience but also bring it to life.


Hobbies: LEGO building, puzzles, board games, DIY, origami, movies, piano, badminton, travel, walking
